The height of the tulip varies from 10 to 100 cm.

The adnexal roots grow from the bottom of the bulb, and they represent the root system of this plant. Each year, these roots die. In young bulbs, hollow stolons appear - these are lateral shoots that can grow both vertically down and to the sides. A daughter bulb appears at the bottom of these shoots. The erect stem has a cylindrical shape. On the surface of the lanceolate-elongated regularly arranged leaflets there is a coating of wax, which is why their color acquires a greenish-bluish tint. At the very top is the smallest sheet plate called the flag sheet. And below is the largest leaf. Typically, a plant has 1 flower. But there are species in which grows from 3 to 5 and even more flowers. They have the right shape. The perianth consists of 6 leaves, while there are as many stamens with anthers of an elongated shape. The most common color of the flower is red, more rare - yellow and very rare - white. Varietal tulips can be painted in a variety of colors. So, purple, yellow, almost black, red, snow-white, purple are found. There are also varieties in which the flowers can be painted in several different color shades, while there are many variations of their combinations. Flowers can also have a very different shape, namely: goblet, oval, star-shaped, cupped, lily-shaped, pion-shaped, fringed, etc. The size of the flowers also depends on the variety. So, there are flowers about 12 centimeters long, while their diameter can vary from 3 to 10 centimeters, and during full disclosure it reaches 20 centimeters. The opening of the buds occurs at a time when the sun is shining in the sky, in the evening they are closed. In cloudy weather, the flowers do not open at all. After the plant fades, a fruit is formed, which is a box with 3 faces. Inside it are triangular brownish-yellow flat seeds.

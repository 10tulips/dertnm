The height of the tulip.

The adnexal roots grow from the bottom of the bulb, and they represent the root system of this plant. Each year, these roots die. In young bulbs, hollow stolons appear - these are lateral shoots that can grow both vertically down and to the sides. A daughter bulb appears at the bottom of these shoots. The erect stem has a cylindrical shape. On the surface of the lanceolate-elongated regularly arranged leaflets there is a coating of wax, which is why their color acquires a greenish-bluish tint. At the very top is the smallest sheet plate called the flag sheet. And below is the largest leaf. Typically, a plant has 1 flower. But there are species in which grows from 3 to 5 and even more flowers. They have the right shape. The perianth consists of 6 leaves, while there are as many stamens with anthers of an elongated shape.
